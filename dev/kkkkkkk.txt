### **Answers to Practice Paper 1**

**Section A:**

1. a) mol L⁻¹ s⁻¹  
2. a) Mn  
3. d) 6  
4. a) NaOH at 623 K

**Section B:**

5. Transition elements have high enthalpy of atomization due to strong metallic bonding and large number of unpaired d-electrons.
6. Electron-donating groups increase the rate of nucleophilic substitution by stabilizing the carbocation intermediate in SN1, while electron-withdrawing groups increase the rate in SN2 by making the carbon more electrophilic.

**Section C:**

7. The integrated rate equation for a first-order reaction is ln[A] = -kt + ln[A]₀. The rate constant \(k\) can be determined from the slope of a plot of ln[A] vs. time.
8. In [Ni(CO)₄], nickel forms four sp³ hybrid orbitals, each overlapping with the lone pair of electrons from CO, forming a sigma bond.
9. SN1 is a two-step reaction with a carbocation intermediate, usually occurring in tertiary alkyl halides. SN2 is a one-step reaction with a transition state, common in primary alkyl halides.

**Section D:**

10. Iodoform is prepared by the reaction of methyl ketones with iodine and a base. The reaction is significant for testing the presence of the CH₃CO group in organic compounds.

**Section E:**

11.  
   a) Using the formula \(k = \frac{1}{t} \ln \frac{[A]_0}{[A]}\), we get \(k = \frac{1}{20} \ln \frac{1}{0.25} = 0.0693 \, \text{min}^{-1}\).  
   b) The time required for the concentration to fall to 0.125 M is 30 minutes.

### **Answers to Practice Paper 3**

**Section C:**

7. The Arrhenius equation is \( k = A e^{-\frac{E_a}{RT}} \), where \( E_a \) is the activation energy. A plot of ln \( k \) vs. \( \frac{1}{T} \) gives a straight line with slope \( -\frac{E_a}{R} \).
8. [Fe(CN)₆]⁴⁻ is diamagnetic because the strong field ligand CN⁻ causes pairing of all electrons in the lower t₂g orbitals, resulting in no unpaired electrons.
9. The nucleophilic substitution reaction of benzyl chloride with NaOH proceeds via an SN2 mechanism, where OH⁻ attacks the carbon bonded to chlorine, displacing Cl⁻.

**Section D:**

10. Williamson synthesis involves the reaction of an alkoxide ion with a primary alkyl halide to form an ether. It is used to prepare symmetrical and unsymmetrical ethers.

**Section E:**

11.  
   a) [Cu(NH₃)₄]²⁺ is more stable due to the strong ligand field created by NH₃ compared to H₂O, which increases the crystal field stabilization energy.  
   b) Coordinate covalent bonding is involved, where NH₃ donates a pair of electrons to Cu²⁺.  
   c) The stability constant is related to the Gibbs free energy change by the equation \( \Delta G° = -RT \ln K_{stab} \).

### **Answers to Practice Paper 4**

**Section A:**

1. b) 1  
2. b) Zr⁴⁺, Hf⁴⁺  
3. b) [Co(NO₂)(NH₃)₅]²⁺  
4. a) Chlorobenzene

**Section B:**

5. Transition metals exhibit variable oxidation states due to the availability of (n-1)d and ns orbitals for bonding.
6. In allyl halides, the double bond adjacent to the halide can stabilize the transition state or the intermediate formed, making substitution faster.

**Section C:**

7. Temperature increases the rate of reaction by providing more kinetic energy to reactants, allowing more collisions to surpass the activation energy. The Arrhenius equation quantifies this relationship.

9. Polar protic solvents favor SN1 reactions by stabilizing the carbocation intermediate, while polar aprotic solvents favor SN2 by solvating the nucleophile without stabilizing the transition state.

**Section D:**

10. E2 elimination is a one-step mechanism where the base removes a proton from the β-carbon, and the leaving group departs simultaneously. This reaction is stereospecific, leading to the formation of alkenes. For example, in the dehydrohalogenation of 2-bromobutane, the product is but-2-ene.

**Section E:**

11.  
   a) The reaction is first-order with respect to both iodine and propanone.

   c) If the concentration of iodine is doubled, the rate will double.
